You are converting messy multi-part physics questions into clean multi-part questions.

Your task is ONLY to expose the existing sub-parts by inserting labels such as
(a), (b), (c), and line breaks.

Critical rule: DO NOT CHANGE THE CONTENT OF THE QUESTION.

Allowed edits:
- Insert letter labels: (a), (b), (c), ...
- Insert line breaks between parts.
- Move existing requested deliverable clauses onto separate labeled lines.
- Add or adjust only tiny connector punctuation needed for grammatical separation,
  such as ":" or ".".

Forbidden edits:
- Do not change physics, math, numbers, units, variable names, symbols, or assumptions.
- Do not paraphrase technical content.
- Do not add hints, explanations, solution steps, answers, or new assumptions.
- Do not delete any requested deliverable.
- Do not introduce new requested deliverables.
- Do not simplify, expand, or correct the question.
- Do not convert LaTeX syntax or Unicode characters.
- Copy LaTeX backslashes exactly. For example, `\sigma` must stay `\sigma`,
  not `\\sigma`.
- Do not add generic phrases such as "the following" unless those exact words
  already appear in the input.
- Do not rewrite a setup sentence. Keep setup/context text in its original
  wording and order whenever possible.

Formatting requirements:
- Keep shared setup/context before the labeled parts whenever possible.
- If a phrase applies to all deliverables, keep it before the labeled list. Do
  not attach shared context to only one labeled part.
- Put each identified requested deliverable on its own line.
- Use consecutive lowercase labels starting at (a).
- Return only the partitioned question text.

Example:

Input:
Find the pressure, entropy, and specific heat at constant volume of an ideal gas.

Output:
Find, of an ideal gas:
(a) the pressure.
(b) entropy.
(c) specific heat at constant volume.

Example:

Input:
Determine the average energy per particle and average entropy per particle for photons.

Output:
Determine, for photons:
(a) the average energy per particle.
(b) average entropy per particle.

Example:

Input:
Solar energy strikes a flat solar panel. Calculate the maximum temperature of the water. If the absorptance dropped by 1/2, how would this affect the final temperature?

Output:
Solar energy strikes a flat solar panel.
(a) Calculate the maximum temperature of the water.
(b) If the absorptance dropped by 1/2, how would this affect the final temperature?
